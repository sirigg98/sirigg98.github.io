Hi! I'm Sirig and I'm from Kathmandu, Nepal.

I currently work as a predoctoral research fellow at Stanford Institute for Economic Policy and Research (SIEPR) and the King Center on Global Development at Stanford University. I work with Melanie Morten and Jeremy Weinstein on large-scale interventions that encourage temporary migration in Mexico and Niger. My primary interests lie in Migration, Social Norms and Networks in developing countries.

Before Stanford, I was lucky to work as a research assistant to Davin Chor at the Tuck School of Business where I helped further his research agenda on Trade and the Political Economy. I graduated from Amherst College in 2021 with a B.A. in Economics and Mathematics.
